I am an Assistant Professor of Mathematical Economics in the [Department of Mathematical Sciences](https://www.math.aau.dk/) at Aalborg University, and a data scientist working at the intersection of econometrics and risk modeling.

My research spans time-series econometrics and climate econometrics, centering on resampling methods for hypothesis testing, high-dimensional model selection, and nonparametric methods, with applications to financial and macroeconomic data. I am particularly interested in developing statistically rigorous, computationally practical tools that hold up under real-world data conditions rather than idealized assumptions.

Alongside this methodological work, I apply state-of-the-art econometric and machine learning techniques to the analysis of financial and macro-financial risk, bridging theoretical statistics with applied questions in economics and finance.

I hold a PhD in Economics from Birkbeck, University of London.
